    Woman found dead in Setai bathtub died of overdose after ordering food

    A married music executive has been pictured for the first time since a 34 year-old Colombian woman was found dead in a Miami hotel room they shared overnight. David Bolno, 46, was snapped in Los Angeles on Thursday afternoon.

    01 Apr 2024, 00:00

    A married music executive has been pictured for the first time since a 34 year-old Colombian woman was found dead in a Miami hotel room they shared overnight. 

    David Bolno, 46, was snapped in Los Angeles on Thursday afternoon. 

    He appeared pensive and wore a bright pink hoodie for the outing, which saw him clutching two bottles of Pepsi Max and using his phone to make a call. 

    Bolno was photographed hours after denying any wrongdoing over the death of aspiring musician Laura Lozano, 34, at The Setai hotel in Miami Beach on March 29.

    The music boss - who has worked with Drake and Post Malone - left the hotel around 4:30am, with Lozano Sterling remaining in the hotel room.

    At 1pm the same day, she was found unresponsive in its bathtub, with an unknown substance also found in the bedroom, a police report obtained by DailyMail.com revealed. 

    Cops are not said to believe the substance discovered was any kind of prescription drug.  

    A cause of death has not yet been disclosed, and Bolno is not considered a person of interest in the case. 

    On Wednesday, Bolno branded Lozano's death a 'terrible tragedy,' but insisted he'd already left when she died.  

    David Bolno looked pensive as he stepped out in Los Angeles on Thursday morning 

    Bolno has denied all claims of wrongdoing, and he is not considered a suspect in Lozano's death 

    Bolno was seen clutching two bottles of Pepsi Max as he stepped outside for air

    At one point, Bolno was seen taking a call, although it's unclear who he was chatting to 

    He spoke hours after DailyMail.com discovered the woman found dead in a bathtub in The Setai hotel after 'making music' with an executive who reps was a Colombian native.

    Lozano Sterling ordered room service after the man left - then died of a suspected drug overdose, DailyMail.com can reveal. 

    Laura Lozano Sterling was found lying face up in the bathtub of room 2308 of The Setai hotel, a luxury, $2,600-a-night suite, at around 1pm on March 30. 

    The room was booked by David Bolno, a 46-year-old business executive and music manager from California. 

    Bolno, who is married, told DailyMail.com that Lozano was a client and that he was helping her career. 

    David Bolno with his wife Stacy Pineles. He has been cleared in the death of the woman who was found dead in the bathtub of his Miami hotel room

    Lauren Lozano Sterling was found dead in a bathtub similar to this one in room 2805 at 1pm on March 30. She had ordered room service earlier that day, after Bolno left the hotel 

    He insisted he 'let her stay' in the room but that there was 'not much more to it' than a late night of work. 

    Bolno left The Setai at 4.35am on the morning of Thursday March 30. 

    It's unclear where he went, but he was seen leaving on hotel surveillance footage cameras. 

    Bolno told DailyMail.com the incident was a 'terrible tragedy'

    While Lozano wasn't discovered until 1pm, DailyMail.com can reveal that she was alive when he left the building - and ordered room service after his departure. 

    She is then believed to have taken a bath, but died as the result of a suspected overdose. 

    It's unclear what kind of drugs were used, but law enforcement sources tell DailyMail.com the drugs were not any kind of prescription pills. 

    While Bolno was initially listed as a person of interest on an initial police report, he is not suspected of having any role in the woman's death. 

    'He has cooperated fully with law enforcement,' a police source said, adding there is no 'foul play' involved. 

    It's unclear how long Lozano, who was described by police sources as being Colombian, had lived in the US but she had permanent resident status. 

    Her family were said to have been 'devastated' by her death. 

    Bolno's wife declined to comment when contacted by DailyMail.com, and he wouldn't go into too much detail about how they ended up in the room together so late. 

    He says she was an aspiring musician and that he arranged for her to have her own room in his hotel room but that there is 'not much more to it'.

    'I really liked her. I was helping her make music... it was a terrible tragedy. 

    The Setai is among Miami's most exclusive hotels. Rooms  run up to $2,600 a night 

    'I had vacated the room in the earlier part of the morning so I wasn't there when they found her.

    'It was really sad, she was someone I really liked, and she was a good person.'

    Bolno who is a partner at NKSFB, the largest business management firm in the country working alongside artists in music, film, and television, said he'd hoped to sign Lozano - a budding musician.

    'She'd been there because she was recording music the night before.

    'She's a client and she was going to sign a record deal with me I helped her make music,' he said.

    'I really don't want to go too deeply into it honestly it was a terrible tragedy.'

    When asked why the 34-year-old had been in his room at such odd hours during the night Bolno said: 'I let her stay there, there's not much more to it' and when asked where he was, he repeated, 'she was found when I'd left.'

    'From what I was told she was found much later in the day,' he said.
