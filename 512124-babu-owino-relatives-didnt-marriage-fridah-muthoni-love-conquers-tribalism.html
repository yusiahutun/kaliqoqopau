    Embakasi East Member of Parliament (MP), Babu Owino, has opened up about his enduring love story with his wife, Fridah Muthoni.

    In an interview on TV 47, the lawmaker spoke highly of his wife, describing her as a remarkable woman who has been his rock and pillar of strength.

    Babu admired Muthoni's intelligence, religious devotion, and unwavering support throughout their relationship.

    "She's been a very good woman, very intelligent. She is a religious lady and very prayerful. She's been my strength and pillar, she's my everything. When the world was falling, she stood by my side. She's never run away. I respect her and I love her so much," he said.

    Babu and Muthoni have been together for 13 years

    Their journey as a couple began 13 years ago when they met during her first year at the University of Nairobi.

    "I met at the University of Nairobi while she was in her first year. She has been my wife for 13 years. I will love her till death do us part," Babu said.

    Defying tribalist criticism

    The couple's intertribal marriage has not been without its challenges.

    Babu revealed that many people disapproved of their union simply because Muthoni did not belong to his Luo tribe.

    However, the parliamentarian firmly believes that love transcends tribal boundaries

    "This is the person that God wanted me to marry. This is the person that God had planned in my life. Love conquers. love conquers tribalism. People have been talking, but it is a decision that I made. The right decision that I made. There is nothing like regret at all," said the father of two.
