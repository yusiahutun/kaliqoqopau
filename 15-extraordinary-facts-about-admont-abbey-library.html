    The Largest Monastic Library in the World

    One of the most remarkable facts about Admont Abbey Library is that it holds the title of being the largest monastic library in the world. With its vast collection of books, it is an architectural and cultural treasure nestled in the beautiful countryside of Admont, Austria.

    Stunning Baroque Architecture

    The library’s architectural design is a stunning example of Baroque style. The grandeur of its marble columns, intricate ceiling frescoes, and ornate detailing make it a true masterpiece of its time.

    Home to Over 200,000 Volumes

    Admont Abbey Library boasts an impressive collection of over 200,000 volumes. From ancient manuscripts to contemporary works, this extensive collection covers a wide range of subjects, making it a paradise for book lovers.

    An Enchanting Manuscript Collection

    Within the library, you can find a treasure trove of valuable manuscripts dating back centuries. These delicate and rare writings provide a glimpse into the past, offering insights into the history and culture of the region.

    Awe-Inspiring Ceiling Frescoes

    The library’s stunning ceiling frescoes, created by the renowned artist Bartolomeo Altomonte, depict themes from human knowledge, making it a visual feast for visitors. The elaborate artwork adds a touch of grandeur to the already breathtaking space.

    A Magnificent Baroque Globus Crusiger

    Admont Abbey Library is also home to an exquisite Baroque Globus Crusiger, a symbol of the Holy Roman Empire. This ornate globe topped with a small cross is a testament to the library’s historical significance.

    An Opulent Library Hall

    The library’s main hall is a sight to behold, with its high ceilings, intricate stucco work, and elegant galleries. The grandeur of the space creates an atmosphere of reverence and intellectual curiosity.

    The Monastery’s Long-Standing Heritage

    Admont Abbey, founded in 1074, has a rich history spanning over a thousand years. The library is a testament to the enduring legacy and dedication of the Benedictine monks who have been its caretakers throughout the centuries.

    A Repository of Knowledge

    Admont Abbey Library serves as a repository of knowledge, preserving centuries’ worth of intellectual contributions. Scholars and researchers can delve into its vast collection and uncover hidden gems that offer valuable insights.

    FAQs

    1. What is the history of Admont Abbey Library?

    The Admont Abbey Library was established in the 11th century by the Benedictine monks. Over the centuries, it has undergone several renovations and expansions, ultimately assuming its current stunning Baroque form in the 18th century.

    2. How many books are there in the library?

    The library houses approximately 70,000 volumes, including rare manuscripts, incunabula, and an extensive collection of books on subjects like theology, philosophy, history, and natural sciences.

    3. Can visitors enter the library?

    Yes, visitors are allowed to enter the library. However, access to some areas might be restricted, and it is advisable to check the visiting hours and guidelines beforehand.

    4. Are there any specific rules or restrictions for visitors?

    Yes, to preserve the library’s precious collection, there are certain rules to be followed by visitors. These include not touching the books, refraining from taking photographs with flash, and maintaining a respectful and quiet demeanor.

    5. What other attractions are there in Admont Abbey?

    Aside from the library, Admont Abbey also boasts other notable features such as the Abbey Museum, which showcases religious art and artifacts, the Baroque Marble Hall, the Abbey Church, and the picturesque Abbey Garden.
