    26 Apr 2024, 00:00

    NBC invited NFL fans to witness 'history being made' by subscribing to the company's streaming platform, where Saturday's Chiefs-Dolphins playoff matchup was shown exclusively.

    Of course, paying a monthly fee for Peacock may not be every NFL fan's idea of history, even if this is the first playoff game to be made available solely through a streaming service.

    'I know the Peacock app deal was confusing but still think it's cool we get to be a part of history,' the Ringer's Ryen Russillo joked on X. 'Thank you NFL owners!'


    'Gotta admit how funny it would be if i paid like $1700 over the course of my life to watch the chiefs lose a playoff game 6-5 because i forgot about my peacock subscription,' Secret Base's Jon Bois added.

    NBC paid a reported $110 million to put the game exclusively on Peacock, which typically charges $5.99 a month for a subscription, but offered a one-year deal for $30 ahead of the Chiefs-Dolphins game.

    Fans had a choice on Saturday: Brave historic cold in Kansas City or pay a fee for Peacock

    The Ringer's Ryen Russillo seized on NBC's bizarre claim that Saturday's game was historic 

    Comedian Bill Burr took aim at NBC's invitation to 'Sign up on Peacock and witness history!'

    Discount aside, fans objected to the move based primarily on principle.

    'Someone is going to convince themselves tonight that it's better to go spend $100 at the bar to watch the Chiefs-Dolphins game for free instead of paying $6/month for Peacock â€” and I respect that,' wrote sports business reporter Joe Pompliano.

    Buffalo Wild Wings, a national chain of sports bars, seized on the opportunity by responding to dozens of complaints on X to remind fans that they can catch all the action down at 'b-dubs.'

    'No to Peacock,' wrote one angry fan. 'Hope their NFL viewership sucks. Getting really tired of being nickel and dimed to death for programming.'

    BWW's social media team wasted little time in responding: 'Yes to watching the game at b-dubs.'

    Buffalo Wild Wings seized on the opportunity to invite fans to watch the game over at 'B-dubs'

    NFL fans weren't alone on Saturday as Swifties also needed to subscribe to Peacock

    Earlier in the week, Democrat congressman Pat Ryan of New York ripped both NBC and NFL commissioner Roger Goodell in an open letter.

    'It's a disgrace,' Ryan wrote in the letter addressed to Goodell and NBC Sports President Rick Cordella. 'Fans already face exorbitant prices to watch every game during the regular season; they don't deserve to be squeezed even further by greedy corporations.

    'This bait-and-switch is particularly egregious for consumers who already pay for NBC as part of their cable package. Since Peacock is a subsidiary of NBCUniversal, this exclusive deal essentially double-charges loyal fans to watch games they thought they had already paid for.

     Goodell (pictured) sold exclusive rights to the Wild Card game for a reported $110million

    Congressman Pat Ryan (left) slammed NBC Sports President Rick Cordella (right) in the letter

    'Enough is enough,' Ryan wrote. 'The NFL raked in $12 billion in 2022, while NBCUniversal made nearly $40 billion. It's time we put power back where it belongs: with the fans.

    'The NFL and NBC must make this right. I'm requesting that you offer the game on broadcast television, end the exclusive and exploitative streaming deals for good, and commit to making games more affordable and accessible for fans to watch throughout the season.'

    Since 1961, the NFL has benefitted from Congress' antitrust exemption with regards to leagues' broadcasting deals, thereby creating the economic landscape that has allowed sports properties to greater and greater media rights deals.

    'Congress granted the NFL an antitrust exemption in its broadcast deals with the expectation that you wouldn't use it to screw over fans,' Ryan wrote. 'That was clearly a mistake.'
