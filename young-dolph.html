    Early Life

    Young Dolph – Net Worth 2024

    Young earned a massive amount of money throughout his singing career. His estimated net worth during his death in 2021 was $3 million.

    Career

    Talking about his career, Young developed a love of music early. When his grandmother died in 2008, he was devastated. To express himself, he turned to music and began rapping.

    In the end, he issued his debut mixtape, “Paper Route Campaign,” to positive reviews. The success of the mixtape inspired him to learn more about music. His other mixtapes and singles over the years include “High-Class Street Music,” “Blue Magic,” “South Memphis Kingpin,” “A Time 2 Kill,” and “Felix Brothers.”

    He also appeared in the hit song “Cut It” by O.T. Genasis from 2015, which peaked at number 35 on the Billboard Hot 100. ‘King of Memphis,’ his debut album, was released in 2016. It was well-received by critics.

    It was also commercially successful, reaching its peak position on the American Billboard 200 at number 49. ‘Let Me See It,’ ‘Royalty,’ and ‘USA’ were among the album’s singles.

    Caption: Young Dolph live performance at halftime of Games 3 Grizzlies-Jazz series source WREG.

    In April 2017, Young released his next album, ‘Bulletproof,’ which peaked at number 36 on the Billboard 200. It had singles, such as ‘But I’m Bulletproof”, ‘100 Shots’, and ‘I Pray for My Enemies’.

    ‘Tracking Numbers’ and ‘Thinking Out Loud’ were released in the same year. The latter reached its highest point at number 16 on the US Billboard 200, while the former failed to chart.

    More about his career

    He recently released the mixtapes “Rich Crack Baby” and “Gelato.” On the US Billboard 200, the former peaked at position 132 while the latter peaked at position 54. Role Model, his fifth studio album, was released in September 2018.

    It rose to number 15 on the US Billboard 200. Further, His seventh album, Rich Slave, which he released in 2020, became his highest-charting work. It reached the fourth spot on the Billboard 200.

    Brand Endorsements

    Young had been the face of many brands throughout his career, ranging from clothing and apparel to restaurants. Some of the brands that Young collaborated with are Avianne Jewelers, Fendi, Calvin Klein, Icebox Diamonds and Watches, and Neff Headwear.

    Personal Life

    Regarding his love life, Young never kept his private affairs a secret from the public.

    He was open about his long-term relationship with Mia Haye, with whom he had two children: a son named Tre Tre Thornton and a daughter named Aria Ella Thornton. 2014 saw the birth of the first child, and 2017 saw the birth of the second.

    Death

    In 2017, Young was the intended victim of a shooting in Charlotte, North Carolina. Due to his bulletproof SUV, he avoided any serious injuries despite being hit by almost 100 bullets.

    Both Dolph and rapper Yo Gotti chose to remain silent despite rumors that their feud had caused the incident. On November 17, 2021, Young passed away following a shooting in Memphis at the age of 36.
