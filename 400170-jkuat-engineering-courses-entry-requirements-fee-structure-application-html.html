    JKUAT diploma courses

    The JKUAT TVET Institute is responsible for offering diploma and certificate programmes. It is worth noting that the institute does not offer engineering courses.

    JKUAT cluster points requirements

    It is important to understand the JKUAT engineering courses and cluster points requirements before applying for admission into the institution. Below are the general entry requirements.

    • A mean grade of C+ in your KCSE
    • A C+ in Physics, Mathematics, Chemistry, and any other subject in groups 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6.
    • If you have a diploma, it should be from KNEC or an approved institution.
    • A pass on your diploma. If you have a credit, you will start learning from 2nd year.
    • Those applying for any Electrical course need a C+ in English or Kiswahili.
    • Those enrolling for JKUAT masters courses must have a first-class or second upper class bachelor's degree in a related field from a recognised institution.
    • PhD applicants must have a master's degree in a related field from a recognised institution.

    JKUAT application procedure

    The university charges a non-refundable application fee of KES 1,500. The amount is payable to the finance office. Follow the steps below to apply for admission into the institution.

    • Make sure you qualify: Before applying, check whether you meet the minimum entry requirements for your preferred course. Note that engineering courses generally require higher grades than humanities and arts. You must also have the correct subject combination.
    • Pay the registration fee: Visit the nearest bank to deposit the amount. The payment details are available on the institution's website. You can also pay the application fee via M-Pesa.
    • Open your browser on an internet-enabled device.
    • Go to the engineering department website: Use the official address.
    • Download the application form: Click on the application form link located in the website's left sidebar. Select the directory you want to save the file if you are using a laptop.
    • Print it: Copy the form to a removable disk and go to the nearest cyber café. Print it in two or more copies.
    • Fill in your details: Give out the correct information regarding your name, date of birth, contact details, and gender.
    • Choose your preferred course: Tick your first choice and enter your second, third, and fourth choices.
    • Fill in your academic details: Clearly capture your qualifications. Remember to support anything you write in this section with certificates or transcripts.
    • Check for errors and omissions: If you have misspelt or omitted anything, start the application afresh on the other copy of the application form you printed.
    • Sign off the letter: Declare that the information provided is correct by appending your signature and date in the given fields.
    • Do not fill in section 7.0: The section is made for official use only. If you fill it, you might be disqualified.
    • Submit the documents: Return the form to the College of Engineering.

    Documents needed

    Make sure you attach the following documents to your application.

    • Original receipt issued after paying the application fee
    • Copies of your certificates or results slip
    • A copy of your birth certificate or ID card

    NB: Successful applicants will get the JKUAT admission letter. The letter will offer more details about the admission process.

    JKUAT fee structure

    The fee structures for self-sponsored, government-sponsored, and international students are different. Generally speaking, international students pay about 20% more than self-sponsored students.

    The annual fee is also different for different levels of academic learning. The fees for undergraduate programmes are less than those charged for master's and PhD courses.
