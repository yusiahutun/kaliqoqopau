    A community has rallied behind a young woman described as a 'full of life' larrikin after a horror crash left her fighting for her life.

    Two weeks ago 22-year-old Macy Edwards was returning to her home in Wynyard, in Tasmania's north-west, when she was involved in a car crash that left her with multiple life-threatening injuries.

    Ms Edwards was travelling from a tour guide training course in Launceston when the collision occurred.

    She now has a significant list of internal injuries, including brain and heart trauma, broken ribs, pelvis, collarbone and neck and tailbone fractures - with some of the injuries requiring treatment in Melbourne when she is stable.

    A horror crash has left 22-year-old Macy Edwards (pictured) fighting for life as her local community in Tasmania rallies behind the 'full of life' larrikin

    Ms Edwards was returning to her home in Wynyard, in Tasmania's north-west, when she was involved in the car crash that left her with multiple life-threatening injuries

    A GoFundMe page has been set up to help financially support Ms Edwards and her family with the cost of her ongoing treatment, raising more than $45,000.

    Page organisers Allie House and Fiona Pearce wrote: 'With a boundless sense of adventure and the world at her fingertips, our wonderful Wynyard girl has remained in an induced coma in Hobart for over a week since her accident.'

    'She is a larrikin who is full of life, has a love of people and is designed and destined for helping others. For those of you who know her parents, Celisa and Steve (Eddie), they are traits she has inherited from them,' they said.

    'This GoFundMe provides us the opportunity to give them precious time to support Macy without the weighty burden of worrying about their lack of income and future needs for treatment and care that will likely ensue.'

    With support pouring in from far and wide, her family thanked everyone for their well wishes.

    With support pouring in from far and wide, her family thanked everyone for their well wishes saying, 'we are so overwhelmed by the kindness of people that brings us to tears' (pictured: Macy (middle) with her family)

    'We are so overwhelmed by the kindness of people that brings us to tears. We didn't expect anything from anyone and just needed to be here for Macy, but this is truly just so heartwarming and a relief during a really tough time,' they wrote.

    'Thank you from the bottom of our hearts.'

    Ms Edwards is currently receiving treatment at Royal Hobart Hospital and the family still does not know the full extent of her injuries and what ongoing support the 22-year-old will require moving forward.
