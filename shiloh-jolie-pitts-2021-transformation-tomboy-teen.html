    What did 2020 look like for LGBT icon Shiloh Jolie-Pitt?

    Wearing a black hoodie and denim cut-off shorts along with black Converse trainers, Shiloh has also shot up in the height department. In fact, her new look could even be considered conventional or stereotypical – at least when contrasted with her old style.

    In a 2008 interview with Oprah Winfrey, Brad opened up about raising Shiloh: “She only wants to be called John. John or Peter. It’s a Peter Pan thing. And then I’ll say, ‘John, would you like some orange juice?’ And she goes, ‘No!’”

    How these celebrity children are breaking gender boundaries

    Angelina told Vanity Fair in a 2010 interview that Shiloh has been exploring gender since age three. “She wants to be a boy,” Jolie said. “So we had to cut her hair. She likes to wear boy clothes. She thinks she’s one of her brothers.”

    Since then, many fans have widely referred to Shiloh as John, with a full Wikipedia page and social fan pages popping up all over the internet. In fact, a search on Google Trends reveals that online users are searching for both “Shiloh” and “John” pretty equally.

    Over the years, Shiloh has also generated buzz with her androgynous, fearless chic style. She was always encouraged by her parents to dress the way she wanted. As a child, she would don casual separates – polo shirts, jackets, blazers, shorts, dark trousers, jeans and oversized tracksuits.

    When Shiloh’s parents would bring her to red carpet premieres and events, she skipped gowns and dresses in favour of suits similar to what her dad wore.

    How these celebrity children are breaking gender boundaries

    So it’s no surprise that followers of the superstar family have noticed the sudden change in Shiloh’s style. “I love this new look! She always looks beautiful with any look though,” commented @dannelope on Instagram. “Good for her or him. Your choice is your own,” said @mcfaddenreid.

    In any case, Shiloh’s had a massive growth spurt and seems to already be towering over her mother. They grow up so fast, eh?
