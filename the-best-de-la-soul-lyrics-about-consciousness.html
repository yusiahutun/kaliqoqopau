    The best De La Soul lyrics about Consciousness

    05 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Consciousness in Music: Exploring De La Soul's LyricsIn the vast realm of music, certain artists have managed to carve their own niche, not just through their catchy beats or innovative soundscapes, but also through the deep and thought-provoking themes they tackle in their lyrics. One such group is De La Soul, a trio of visionary hip-hop artists known for their conscious approach to music. By delving into the depth of their lyrics, we can explore the recurring theme of consciousness and its role in their artistry.1) "Me Myself and I" - Released in 1989, this iconic track from De La Soul's debut album, "3 Feet High and Rising," showcases their ability to express individuality and self-awareness. Here, the group emphasizes the importance of embracing one's true self through lyrics like "Mirror, mirror on the wall/ Tell me, mirror, what is wrong?" This introspective angle encourages listeners to reflect on their own journey towards self-acceptance and personal consciousness.2) "Eye Know" - The second track from the same album reflects the significance of true connection amidst the noise of superficial relationships. This love-infused song emphasizes the need to recognize and cherish genuine connections rather than succumbing to shallow interactions. De La Soul's line, "Yes, you're on my mind right now /Your love is soooo, ohhh, sooo jazzy" highlights their profound understanding of emotional intimacy and the desire to foster authentic connections.3) "Stakes Is High" - Released in 1996, the title track of De La Soul's fourth studio album serves as a social commentary on the state of society. This thought-provoking song addresses various issues such as violence, materialism, and corruption, urging listeners to recognize the harsh realities of the world. With lyrics like "No matter how much we've grown/Or set aside our own/It's a fact, yo, that our music is all wack," the group challenges the mainstream narrative and encourages individuals to question the societal norms they blindly adhere to.4) "Much More" - This introspective track from De La Soul's 2004 album, "The Grind Date," exemplifies their conscious approach to self-reflection and personal growth. In "Much More," the group acknowledges the challenges and temptations they face but ultimately emphasize the importance of staying true to oneself. With the lines, "Now listen up, I got a story to tell / A version of my ghetto where life felt for real," De La Soul invites listeners to engage in their stories and experiences, evoking a sense of shared consciousness.5) "Pain" - From their 1991 album, "De La Soul Is Dead," this track delves into the struggles of everyday life and the importance of perseverance. Through poignant lyrics like "Broken glass and rusty nails where/Desperation follows, you grope your way through the dark," the group sheds light on the obstacles and pain that often accompany personal growth and consciousness. It serves as a reminder that despite the hardships, one can navigate through darkness and eventually find solace.6) "Against the World" - A 2016 release from De La Soul's album "and the Anonymous Nobody...," this song further emphasizes the group's commitment to consciousness and their willingness to stand up against injustice. With lyrics such as "Fightin' for freedom, let's everybody die for somethin' / Stand for somethin', don't give up the fight for nothin'," De La Soul encapsulates the spirit of resistance and the importance of a collective consciousness towards societal issues.Throughout their extensive discography, De La Soul consistently displays a conscious approach to their lyrics, offering a diverse range of perspectives that encourage self-reflection, social awareness, and personal growth. By embedding profound themes into their music, they enable listeners to explore the depths of consciousness, urging them to question societal norms, embrace their true selves, and strive for a better world. In a world inundated with mindless popular culture, De La Soul's conscious message remains a beacon of enlightenment within the realm of music.
