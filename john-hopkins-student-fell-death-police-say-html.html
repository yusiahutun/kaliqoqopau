    John Hopkins student fell to her death Baltimore PD says

    Abigail Bastien, 19, was found dead outside her apartment near the Johns Hopkins University campus in Baltimore Thursday morning A Johns Hopkins University student athlete who was found dead outside of her off-campus apartment building last week appears to have accidentally fallen off the building.

    06 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Abigail Bastien, 19, was found dead outside her apartment near the Johns Hopkins University campus in Baltimore Thursday morning 

    A Johns Hopkins University student athlete who was found dead outside of her off-campus apartment building last week appears to have accidentally fallen off the building. 

    Baltimore Police said Monday that 19-year-old Abigail Bastien's death appears to be the result of an accidental fall.

    After a preliminary investigation, police spokesman T.J. Smith said that 'nothing in this case points towards homicide'.  

    'Our early indications, based on all the evidence that we have, which includes video evidence and statements from people, is that this is an accidental death where it appears that she fell off of the building from quite a distance and died as a result of that,' Smith said, according to the Baltimore Sun. 

    'We’re still waiting on toxicology results from the medical examiner’s office before we’re able to determine exactly everything that was going on.'

    Bastien was found just outside of the John Hopkins Homewood Campus North (pictured left) on the 3700 block of N Charles Street (street view right). Police now believe that the track star accidentally fell to her death

    Police are waiting for toxicology reports. Above, Bastien in a picture from Twitter

    Bastien was a sprinter on the track and field team at Johns Hopkins

    Police said they do not know how Bastien, a sprinter on the track team, fell, or from where in or on the building. 

    However, they are now more certain about what 'occurred during the period of time leading up to her death,' thanks to video surveillance and witnesses. 

    Bastien's body was found Thursday morning around 8am outside her apartment building on the 3700 block of N Charles Street,  just north of the university's Homewood campus.

    A prayer service in Bastien's honor was held last Thursday evening at St. Mary's Catholic Church in her home town of Sandusky, Ohio

    Friends and classmates of student Abigail Bastien pray together at St. Mary's Catholic Church Thursday evening in her home town of Sandusky, Ohio

    Bastien, who was from Sandusky, Ohio, was in her sophomore year and was majoring in molecular and cell biology. She was working in the cell biology department in the university's School of Medicine and at THB Bagels & Deli over the summer.

    The sophomore was also a member of the Alpha Phi sorority and was a star sprinter on the track and field team.
