    Ugo Humbert | Career

    Junior

    When Ugo turned 12, he left his home and marched to Poitiers to train with the French Tennis Federation at the National Training Centre.

    He actually continued his training there, but he faced some injuries that stopped him from playing for a year and a half.

    Later in 2015, Ugo got a chance to play in the Abierto Juvenil Mexicano doubles final long with the tennis player Geoffrey Blancaneaux. His career-high junior ranking is No. 18, attained in January 2016.

    2017: First Futures title

    In September 2017, Ugo acquired his first futures title in Bagnères-de-Bigorre, where he was spotted like a wild card entry.

    Following a week, he again made a wild card entry into the Moselle Open, where he made it to the second round but lost three sets to Simone Bolelli.

    During the 2017 Rolex Paris Masters, Ugo notched his first win against Thomas Fabbiano, a top 100 player with jersey number 73. It was a first-round qualifying match.

    2018: Entering the top 100

    Ugo could not make it to the level as he was expected to in the first half of the season on the ATP Challenger Tour.

    But later on, he achieved a breakthrough season over the summer when he reached three Challenger finals.

    He lost the first two in Gatineau and Granby, but Ugo notched his maiden Challenger title in Segovia.

    The game certified him for the US Open qualifying tournament, where he made his first spot in his first single Grand Slam main draw.

    In October, he notched his second Challenger title in Ortisei, beating Pierre-Hugues Herbert, world number 55. And for himself, he made it to a career-high of world No. 99.

    2019: Wimbledon fourth round

    Ugo started his 2019 season by certifying for ATP 250 Brisbane and lost to Yasutaka Uchiyama in the round of 32.

    The next week, he opted to play for ATP 250 Auckland. And this was when he made a win against Janik Sinner, Herbert Hurkacz, and Benoit Paire to get the title.

    Not only that, he became the first Frenchman to win this tournament and the youngest Frenchman to win an ATP title since Gasquet in 2007.

    Ugo also proved his performance best, recorded his best Grand Slam result and made it to the fourth round of Wimbledon in 2019.

    He knocked out compatriot Gael Monfils and Felix Auger-Aliassime but eventually lost to the winner Novak Djokovic.

    2020-Present

    At the 2020 Hamburgh European Open, Ugo defeated world no. 5, Daniil Medvedev, in the first round, which became the biggest highlight of his career.

    But later, he was defeated in the quarterfinals by Casper Ruud. Likewise, he also got eliminated in the first round at the French Open by Marc Polmans.

    In the 2021 season at the Murray River Open, Ugo faced James Duckworth but lost in the second round.

    Likewise, at the Madrid Open, also Ugo lost in the first round to Aslan Karatsev. Further in Rome, he was defeated by Jannik Sinner, that too in the first round.
