    Flour taco shells have a higher calorie content.

    Compared to corn taco shells, flour taco shells tend to have a higher calorie count due to the addition of ingredients such as wheat flour and oil.

    FAQs

    1. How many calories are in a taco shell?

    A typical taco shell contains around 60-70 calories. However, the calorie content may vary depending on the size and brand of the taco shell.

    2. Are taco shells high in carbohydrates?

    Taco shells do contain carbohydrates, as they are primarily made from flour or corn. The carbohydrate content ranges from 10-15 grams per shell.

    3. Can I eat taco shells on a low-fat diet?

    Yes, taco shells can be consumed as part of a low-fat diet. Opt for baked or grilled taco shells instead of fried ones to reduce the fat content.

    4. Are there any gluten-free options available for taco shells?

    Yes, corn taco shells are typically gluten-free. However, always check the label or ingredients list to ensure they are free from any gluten-containing additives.

    5. Can I make my own taco shells?

    Absolutely! Making your own taco shells can be a fun and healthier alternative. Use whole wheat or corn tortillas, and shape them into taco shells before baking them in the oven until crisp.
