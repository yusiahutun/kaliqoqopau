    List of Kenyan Billionaires Who Excelled in Their Studies, Businesses They Own

    A popular phrase in Kenya says education is the key to success.

    A few Kenyans who excelled in class have also made it big in life, establishing multi-billion businesses.

    TUKO.co.ke looks at successful entrepreneurs who topped in class and joined the billionaires league according to Who Owns Kenya.

    1. James Mwangi

    Equity Bank boss James Mwangi was born in 1962 in a tiny village called Nyagatugu, flanking the Aberdare Ranges in Central Kenya and was raised by a peasant mother after his father died shortly after his birth.

    His rise to a world-class banker was shaped by the challenges he faced as a young boy who herded cows and goats in a communal atmosphere and sold milk in the village, getting a first-hand experience in the business.

    "It was a simple life. As boys, we grazed cows and goats...we hunted wild animals like rabbits. Growing up, we didn't know if people were well off or poor; we were all equals. As a community, we built houses together and socilialised at village dances," he told Forbes Africa Magazine.

    Mwangi began his education at Nyagatugu Primary School in Kangema. He proceeded to Ichagaki Secondary School and later Kagumo High School for his A-level education.

    He emerged as one of the top students in Kagumo, and after excelling, he joined the University of Nairobi to pursue a bachelor of commerce degree.

    Mwangi has over 185 million shares at Equity, representing a 5% stake in the lender.

    2. Jaswinder Bedi

    Bedi is the chairperson of the Kenya Export Promotion and Branding Agency.

    He is also a board member of the Industrial and Commercial Development Corporation (ICDC).

    The businessman studied textile technology at Bradford College in the United Kingdom before proceeding to the prestigious Harvard University Business School.

    The managing director of Bedi Investments' net worth is estimated at $680 million (KSh 79 billion) according to the Inequality Kills report by Oxfam.

    3. Josphat Mwaura

    Mwaura was among the top students at Starehe Boys Centre.

    The former KPMG chief executive officer studied accountancy at Strathmore University.

    In 2002, he joined the Kennedy School of Government at Harvard University, where he studied a macroeconomic program on management.

    He currently serves as a director at Starehe Boys on a volunteer basis.

    4. Chris Kirubi

    The late industrialist was among the top students at Friends School, Kamusinga.

    As a bright student, he was admitted to the prestigious Harvard Business School and later to Handel’s University in Sweden.

    He bagged his master's degree from INSEAD –a non-profit business school with campuses in Europe.

    Kirubi, who died on June 14, 2021, served as the director at Centum Investment and the chairman of Capital Group Limited, which owns Capital FM.

    He was a proprietor of a handful of other businesses and properties, including DHL Express Kenya Limited, Haco Industries Kenya Limited, and Kiruma International Limited.

    He left behind wealth worth over KSh 20 billion.

    5. Philip Ndegwa

    Kenya's third Central Bank of Kenya governor Philip Ndegwa was reportedly the first Kenyan to 'bank a billion'.

    Ndegwa studied at Alliance High School before joining Makerere University, where the late president Mwai Kibaki was an alumnus.

    The former PS co-founded First Chartered Securities alongside other 20 civil servants in 1974.

    The Ndegwa family owns an 80% stake in ICEA Lion Group which announced it had crossed the KSh 100 billion mark in asset base in 2021.

    Kenyan billionaires whose education ended at Primary level

    Renowned tycoons whose education ended at primary school include Jackson Kibor, Andrew Ngirici and Narendra Raval.

    Andrew Ngirici, the husband to Kiriyaga woman rep Purity Ngirici reportedly dropped out of school while in Class Four at Kirigo Primary School.

    The late controversial Uasin Gishu tycoon Jackson Kibor accumulated wealth worth over KSh 6 billion despite his little educational background.
