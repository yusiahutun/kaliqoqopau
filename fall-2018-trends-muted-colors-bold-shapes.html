    During fall, life is often rendered in a desaturated palette. As the sky cycles from electric blue to steely pewter, our wardrobes, too, transform. Layers of velvet, tweed and fleece replace airy linen and silk, and vibrant neons melt into deep shades of gold, olive and rust. But as we turn down the volume on our palettes, we turn up the volume on our silhouettes.

    Chilly days and muted hues invite us to play with structure, texture and weight. A limited color range imposes a creativity that underpins the year’s coldest months, proving that far from being blah, neutrals, earth shades and jewel tones can be just as captivating as bright ones.
