    Last week, Kate Moss teased the unveiling of her wellness brand, Cosmoss, positioned as "self-care created for life's modern journeys". Launching today at cosmossbykatemoss.com, it "draws on the extraordinary life experience of Kate Moss — ready to share her journey of self-acceptance and freely be herself".

    The move comes after the British supermodel and pop culture icon has branched out into other areas of business, representing talent – including her daughter Lila – at her eponymous agency conceived in 2016, as well as becoming the UK's creative director of Diet Coke earlier this year.

    Cosmoss

    While this is her first venture into the commercial world of wellness, Moss has openly spoken about her love of daily yoga for some time now, being tutored by Nadia Narain – one of London's most celebrated yoga teachers and the author of multiple self-care books. Moss also famously frequents TheLifeCo Bodrum, a wellbeing and detox retreat; and at home is a fan of juicing (so she told The Guardian).

    Related Story

    Still, Moss is as synonymous with a party-hard lifestyle as she is with fashion, but Cosmoss may well challenge the notion that wellbeing and hedonism can't coexist. It's said to be "a celebration of every day exactly as it is, with all its imperfections," seemingly removing the virtue from popularised self-care.

    "Each product has been meticulously crafted with wellbeing in mind; each ritual opens a door to balance, restoration, and love," says Moss. The line-up reveals a Golden Nectar CBD and Pro-Collagen Oil, Face Cream, Face Cleanser, Sacred Mist fragrance, and Dawn and Dusk Teas.

    CBD and bakuchiol (a plant alternative to retinol) are the star ingredients within the skincare products, offering support for stressed complexions. Meanwhile, the fragrance mist sees an aromatic blend of bergamot, tuberose and grounding woods to uplift the spirits. The tea, rare in a celebrity-fronted line-up, makes perfect sense here as it completes the whole self-care ritual that the brand prides itself on.

    As Moss tells Bazaar, she looked close to home when creating the products. "I started spending a lot of time in my garden in the pandemic and noticing flowers that I didn't even know I had, it's become my favourite place now, where I feel happiest," she exclusively reveals. "I go out into the field and meditate sometimes early in the morning before the world is awake."
