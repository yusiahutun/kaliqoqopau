    Caspar Lee Bio, Age

    YouTuber, Casper Lee was born in Paddington London on 24 April 1994. After his birth, his mother brought him to South Africa where he lived with his family, his parents, and his older sister Theodora. When he was six years old, he was diagnosed with Tourettes Syndrome – a neurological disorder characterized by repetitive, stereotypical, involuntary movements, and vocalizations called tics.

    During his stay in South Africa, his family moved to different parts of the country where he had the opportunity to experience many facets of life and learn some life-long lessons. First, he went to a rural area called Fort Nottingham and then to Durban, South Africa, where he attended an all-boys school, Crawford College, La Lucia.

    At the age of 11, he had to move again to the Western Cape where he learned to play tennis. He also attended Oakhill High School in Knysna. A few years later his parents, Emily Riordan Lee and Jonathan Lee, divorced, which does not bode well for him, but he was able to build a strong relationship with his parents afterward.

    Caspar started his YouTube channel in 2011 under the name Dicasp (Director Caspar) but was later renamed, Caspar. His channel quickly became quite popular and he started working with other famous YouTube personalities such as Joe Sugg (with whom he later shared an apartment), Jacks’s Gap, Sam Pepper, Marcus Butler, and others. The collaboration helped to increase the number of his subscribers to the point where it became a career. He even launched another channel, morecaspar, where he uploads funny vlogs.

    However, he started migrating again and in December 2012 moved back to his birthplace, London, England, where he lived with Alfie Deyes, a YouTuber colleague, until 2014. Shortly thereafter, he moved in with another YouTuber friend, Joe Sugg (ThatcherJoe), when his former roommate moved in with his girlfriend Zoe Sugg aka Zoella (Joe’s sister who lived with him). He stayed with Joe until February 2016, when he moved into a shared apartment with Josh Pieters, also a YouTube.

Along with a number of other YouTubers, he starred in the 2014 Style Haul series The Crew and was featured in an episode of the 2014 American television series Web Therapy.
