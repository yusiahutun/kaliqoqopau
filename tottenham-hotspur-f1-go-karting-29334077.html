    30 Jul 2024, 00:00

    Tottenham Hotspu r have teamed up with F1 to open up "London’s longest indoor electric" go-kart track underneath their stadium.

    The track will be located underneath the South Stand - and Spurs noted it could be a future venue for the national karting championship. There will be two tracks built, one for adults and one for juniors - both accredited by the National Karting Association.

    Spurs said the track will "create apprenticeship and career opportunities for local young people and bring greater diversity to the motorsport industry, particularly for women and underrepresented groups.

    "It will also provide F1 with a touchpoint in London from which to further grow its fanbase throughout the world.

    "A driver academy programme will widen the motorsport talent pool and help to identify the next generation of F1 drivers. The Club and F1 will deliver educational activities for schools to engage local young people in motorsport and showcase career opportunities and pathways."

    While Stefano Domenicali, President and CEO at Formula 1, spoke of his excitement about the project.

    Spurs have showcased their new ground as a multi-purpose stadium in recent years - also hosting musical events and the NFL. The experience will also add to the numerous activities already on offer - including the Dare Skywalk, which involves walking along the roof of the stadium.

    However, it didn't take long for opposition fans to take digs at the project, with one writing: "Spurs have added everything to their stadium over the last decade but trophies."
