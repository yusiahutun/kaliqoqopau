    Prince's fortune battle rages on as TWO more people come forward claiming to be heirs

    Two more people have come out of the woodwork, claiming to to be heir of some of Prince's fortune. Prince died suddenly on April 21 and had no recognized children or will even though his estate may be worth hundreds of millions of dollars and includes vast vaults of unreleased recordings.

    06 Sep 2024, 00:00

    Two more people have come out of the woodwork, claiming to to be heir of some of Prince's fortune.

    Prince died suddenly on April 21 and had no recognized children or will even though his estate may be worth hundreds of millions of dollars and includes vast vaults of unreleased recordings. 

    Convict Norman Yates Carthens filed court papers in Carver County Monday claiming to be Prince's adopted son.

    Padden said that Prince's half-brother Duane Nelson Sr also has a surviving daughter, who lives in Wisconsin, meaning she would be the singer's half-niece

    Prince died suddenly on April 21 and had no recognized children or will even though his estate may be worth hundreds of millions of dollars ©Mark Ralston (AFP/File)

    Carthens, who is currently in prison, also said that Prince did leave a will, of which he was left $7 million, according to paperwork obtained by ABC News. 

    But the court filing left no details to support the claims and Prince's sister Tyka, and his estate have already said there is no will.

    Meanwhile, an alleged half-sibling named Regina Sorenson has also filed documents saying she and Prince had the same father, reports TMZ.

    Sorenson claims there is a 'trust' and that she should be included in any DNA testing of potential heirs.

    The pair are among many scrambling to get their hands on the pop legend's wealth and Carthens is not the only convict laying claim to Prince.

    In addition to members of Prince's extended family, a 39-year-old inmate in prison for gun possession in a stolen car is seeking recognition as Prince's son.

    The mother of Carlin Williams said she had sex with Prince in a Kansas City hotel in 1976.

    Williams is also a little-known rapper who has written songs with scenarios in which he kills the 'Purple Rain' star.

    Meanwhile in May, two new suitors to Prince's estimated $300million fortune were named in paperwork filed by an attorney who used to represent the singer's half-brother.

    The mother of Carlin Williams (pictured left and right) said she had sex with Prince in a Kansas City hotel in 1976 

    Meanwhile in May, two new suitors to Prince's estimated $300million fortune were named in paperwork filed by an attorney who used to represent the singer's half-brother, Duane Nelson (right)

    Attorney Michael Padden has filed papers on behalf of two more potential heirs to Prince's fortune, including Victoria Nelson, a minor, whose existence was previously revealed by Dailymail.com (left and right)

    Michael Padden, former lawyer for Duane Nelson Sr who was close to Prince for almost half of his life and died in 2011, said Nelson has two living heirs with a claim on the artist's riches.

    Padden claims Nelson has an adult daughter named Brianna who is possibly living somewhere in Wisconsin, and a granddaughter by his son, Duane Nelson Jr.

    While Padden only names the girl as V.N. in his filing, Dailymail.com previously revealed the existence of that granddaughter whose full name is Victoria Nelson.

    Victoria is the daughter of Duane Nelson Jr, making her Prince's step-grandniece, while Brianna would be the singer's step-niece.

    Padden noted that Duane Nelson Sr is not currently listed among beneficiaries to Prince's due to questions over the identity of his father.

    Prince is known to have one sister, Tyka Nelson, but under Minnesota law his half-brothers and sisters also have a claim to his estimated $300million fortune

    If Padden's claims are true, then the singer's estate could be split up to eight ways, meaning each of his heirs would get aroundb $37.5million each (Tyka Nelson, pictured)

    Prince is known to have five half-brothers and sisters, Sharon, Norrine (pictured left) and John Nelson, Omarr Baker and Alfred Jackson

    But if courts were to rule that he is entitled to a portion of Prince's estate, then that would put Brianna and Victoria, a minor, in line to inherit. Both Duane Nelson Sr and Jr are already dead.

    Under Minnesota law, half-siblings have as much claim on an estate as full siblings, meaning the fortune could be split between up to eight different people.

    Padden said Duane Sr considered himself to be Prince's brother, and Duane Sr also served as a security guard at the artist's Paisley Park estate.

    However the pair became estranged following an argument during which Duane Sr was fired.

    Padden told the New York Daily News: 'They had a falling out and were estranged, but that doesn't change the fact that for 30-plus years, they presented themselves as brothers.'

    Prince is known to have one full-sister, Tyka Nelson, and five half-brothers and sisters, Sharon, Norrine and John Nelson, Omarr Baker and Alfred Jackson.

    However, between 600 and 700 people are estimated to have come forward claiming a share of his considerable fortune following his death on April 21 after he left no will. 

    Prince's half-siblings (pictured above is Alfred Jackson) have been in Minnesota to handle their brother' assents following his death without a will

    Harvey Morse, who works for the company appointed to sift through claims, says most of them are bogus, but many of them will have to be painstakingly checked to see if they are true. 

    Judge Kevin Eade, who is overseeing the estate, issued an order in Carver County, Minnesota, allowing for DNA samples to be taken from Prince's blood to help verify claims, giving those who believe they are related four months in which to file.

    Prince's estate is estimated to be worth $300million, meaning Victoria and the seven others would each get around $37.5million.

    But that is expected to balloon with the after-death sales of his hits and the discovery of a giant trove of previously unreleased songs that the singer locked away in an underground vault.

    Heir Hunters International, another company that specializes in finding long-lost relatives has claimed that it has found Prince's son, but Morse said he doubts that is true.

    'If that was the case I would have expected them to have been in court on Monday establishing their case, but they weren't there. We were,' he said.

    Morse said Prince's estate could take years to sort out. 'Bob Marley also died without a will,' he pointed out. 'He died 35 years ago and settlement of his estate is still ongoing.' 

    Prince was found collapsed in an elevator at Paisely Park before being pronounced dead age 57.
